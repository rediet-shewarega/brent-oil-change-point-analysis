# Assumptions and Limitations

## Assumptions

1. Major geopolitical and economic events may be reflected as structural breaks in Brent oil prices.
2. Brent oil price behavior can be studied using time series methods such as trend analysis, stationarity testing, volatility analysis, and change point detection.
3. Log returns may provide a more stable series for analyzing short-term shocks and volatility.
4. Event dates are approximate because markets may react before or after official announcements.
5. A change point close to an event may suggest association, but not automatic causation.

## Limitations

1. The analysis uses Brent oil prices only and does not include other explanatory variables such as GDP, inflation, exchange rates, inventory levels, production volumes, or demand indicators.
2. Multiple events may occur close together, making it difficult to isolate the effect of one event.
3. Detecting a statistical change point does not prove that a specific event caused the change.
4. Some market reactions may happen before the official event date due to expectations or rumors.
5. A simple change point model may detect only one structural break, while real oil markets may have many overlapping regimes.
